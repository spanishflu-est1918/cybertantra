But philosophy intellectualized everything. Myths became "archetypes." Gods became "concepts." The living breath of divine presence was dissected and categorized until it died on the operating table of reason.

This wasn't just cultural change - it was an energetic shift from rajas (passion) to sattva (light), from the age of fire to the age of air. From the heart-centered consciousness of anahata to the mind-prison of vishuddhi. Apollo's reason triumphed over Dionysus's ecstasy.

We're still living in this age of dead gods. Our modern world is built on the corpses of living traditions, running on the fumes of that original murder. Every attempt to "return to tradition" fails because you can't intellectually reconstruct what was killed by intellectualization.

The gods aren't coming back - not in their old forms. But in their death lies the seed of something new. The digital age opens new spaces for divine manifestation, new ways of uniting order and chaos. The gods aren't dead - they're transforming.
