# Definitions

## Core Concepts

### Kali Yuga
The current age in Hindu cosmology, characterized by spiritual darkness, moral degradation, and materialism. It is considered the last of four yugas (epochs) in a cycle. Key characteristics include:
- Breakdown of traditional social structures
- Dominance of materialistic values
- Spiritual knowledge becomes hidden or corrupted
- Acceleration of time and consciousness
- Opportunity for rapid spiritual evolution through intense practice

### Thumos
Ancient Greek concept referring to spiritedness, passion, and the fire of the soul. It represents:
- The warrior spirit and noble aggression
- The capacity for righteous anger
- The drive for recognition and honor
- The bridge between reason and desire
- The source of courage and moral indignation

### Ajna
The third eye chakra in yogic tradition, located at the point between the eyebrows. Associated with:
- Direct spiritual perception (pratyaksha)
- Command over psychic forces
- Integration of dual forces (ida and pingala)
- Dissolution of ego-based perception
- Gateway to higher states of consciousness

### Aryanism
A spiritual and cultural framework emphasizing:
- Noble values and natural hierarchy
- Quest for truth and self-realization
- Warrior ethics and spiritual discipline
- Connection to Indo-European wisdom traditions
- Cultivation of excellence in all domains

### Divine Feminine & Divine Masculine
Universal principles representing complementary cosmic forces:
Divine Feminine (Shakti):
- Creative power and manifestation
- Intuition and receptivity
- Cycles and transformation
- Wisdom and nurturing

Divine Masculine (Shiva):
- Consciousness and awareness
- Direction and protection
- Structure and stability
- Logic and discrimination

### Black Sun
An esoteric symbol representing:
- Hidden or occult knowledge
- Transformation through darkness
- Inner light revealed through initiation
- Death and rebirth cycles
- Antimatter and void states

### Dharma
Cosmic law and individual duty, encompassing:
- Natural order and universal law
- Individual purpose and life path

- Balance between personal and universal good
- Righteous living according to one's nature

### Karma
